## Executive Summary
 
*The memo everyone was waiting for.*
 
Cubert is a community-driven project built around one of the most universal experiences in modern professional life: corporate burnout.
 
While most crypto projects compete on technical complexity, Cubert competes on **cultural relevance**.
 
The thesis is simple: corporate frustration is one of the most shared, most relatable, and most underserved identities on the internet. Millions of professionals sit in the same meetings, file the same reports, and survive the same performance reviews — across every geography, industry, and language. That shared experience is not just a feeling. It is a community waiting to be built.
 
Cubert exists to build it.
 
Our mission: to become the internet's home for office workers, spreadsheet warriors, meeting survivors, KPI victims, and everyone navigating the complexity of modern corporate life — and to do it through **humor, transparency, decentralized ownership, and a token called $CUBE**.

## The Corporate Burnout Thesis
 
*The market research nobody asked for but everyone needed.*
 
The modern professional experience is defined by a paradox: people are more connected, more productive, and more scrutinized than ever — and more exhausted because of it.
 
The symptoms are well-documented across industries and borders:
 
- Excessive meetings that could have been emails
- Endless reporting cycles that measure everything except morale
- Unrealistic deadlines delivered with the confidence of someone who won't be executing them
- Bureaucracy that outlives the problems it was designed to solve
- Burnout that arrives quietly and leaves loudly
These are not niche complaints. They are a shared language — one that travels across industries, income levels, job titles, and continents. Corporate frustration is one of the most universally relatable forms of modern internet culture. It has generated memes, subreddits, entire genres of workplace humor, and a collective vocabulary that anyone who has ever attended a "quick sync" immediately recognizes.
 
**Cubert exists to turn that shared experience into a community — and that community into something that outlasts the Monday morning standup.**

## Tokenomics
 
*The financial model, finally approved by the committee.*
 
Cubert's token model ($CUBE, Solana SPL) is designed around four principles: **transparency, sustainability, alignment, and verifiability**.
 
| Principle | What it means in practice |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| Transparency | All allocations are public. No hidden wallets. No hidden taxes. |
| Sustainability | The majority of supply (70%) goes to liquidity — not insiders. |
| Alignment | Team tokens have a 6-month cliff followed by 12-month linear vesting. No one profits from a quick exit. |
| Verifiability | Every commitment is backed by on-chain proof, not a PDF. |
 
Key parameters at a glance:
 
| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Token | $CUBE |
| Blockchain | Solana |
| Standard | SPL Token (Classic) |
| Total Supply | 1,000,000,000 |
| Buy / Sell Tax | 0% / 0% |
| Liquidity Pool | 70% — locked at launch via Streamflow (12 months minimum) |
| Corporate Treasury | 15% — multisig controlled (Squads Protocol, 2-of-3) |
| Community Growth Fund | 10% — community campaigns, events, partnerships |
| Team Allocation | 5% — 6-month cliff, 12-month vesting via Streamflow |
 
For complete allocation details, mechanisms, and on-chain verification links, see the [Tokenomics](../tokenomics/) document.

## Governance
 
*How decisions get made without a standing Friday sync.*
 
Cubert operates with a three-tier governance structure:
 
| Tier | Role | Authority |
|------|------|-----------|
| Founder | Final decision authority | Strategy, brand, launch, treasury, Charter amendments |
| Core Contributors | Trusted collaborators | Elevated input, moderation, community review |
| Community | The Corporate Survivors | Voice through proposals, polls, and participation |
 
Governance is designed to evolve. In the current phase, the Founder holds final authority — not as a permanent power structure, but as a practical necessity for an early-stage project where speed and consistency matter. As the community matures and Core Contributors emerge, decision-making will progressively decentralize.
 
The long-term vision includes formal community proposals, transparent treasury oversight, multi-signature controls across independent parties, and governance mechanisms that reflect the size and maturity of the ecosystem.
 
For the complete governance framework, proposal process, and decision timelines, see the [Governance Charter](../governance/).

## Risk Considerations
 
*What the small print says, in large print.*
 
Participation in Cubert involves significant risk. The project is transparent about this because obscuring risk is itself a risk.
 
Key considerations include:
 
- **Market volatility** — $CUBE may lose all value
- **Regulatory uncertainty** — treatment of meme coins varies by jurisdiction and may change
- **Community growth risk** — community-driven projects depend on participation that cannot be guaranteed
- **Liquidity risk** — trading markets for $CUBE may be illiquid or inaccessible
- **Technology risk** — Solana, SPL infrastructure, and third-party platforms (Streamflow, Squads) carry inherent smart contract risk
- **Founder risk** — the project currently operates with a solo Founder, with associated key-person risk
This is not an investment. $CUBE carries no expectation of profit derived from the efforts of the Founder or any contributor.
 
For complete risk details, see the [Risk Disclosure](../RISKS.md) document. Please read it before acquiring or interacting with $CUBE.
