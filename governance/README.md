# 🏛️ Governance Charter
 
> *"This document was written during a meeting that could have been an email.*
> *It governs all future meetings that should also have been emails."*
 
**Filing Ref.:** GOV-001  
**Effective Date:** Upon commit to `main`   
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Active 🟢
 
This Charter defines how CubertCoin is governed: how decisions are made, how the community participates, how priorities are set, and how this document itself can be changed.
 
CubertCoin has no board of directors, no C-suite, no mandatory team-building retreats, and no standing Friday syncs. What it does have is a structure — because even Corporate Survivors need to know who signs off on the memo.
 
---
 
## 🏢 Article I — Organizational Structure
 
*The org chart nobody printed but everyone needed.*
 
### 1.1 The Three Tiers
 
CubertCoin operates with three tiers of authority:
 
| Tier | Title | Role |
|------|-------|------|
| **Tier 1** | Founder | Final decision authority on all matters |
| **Tier 2** | Core Contributors | Trusted collaborators with elevated input on decisions |
| **Tier 3** | Community | The Corporate Survivors — voice through participation |
 
There are no middle managers. There are no quarterly performance reviews. There are no skip-level meetings.
 
### 1.2 The Founder
 
The Founder holds final authority over:
 
- Project direction and strategy
- Official communications and announcements
- Brand integrity and asset usage
- Token launch decisions
- Treasury allocation
- Amendments to this Charter
The Founder is not a dictator — they are the last person left in the room after everyone else filed out.
 
### 1.3 Core Contributors
 
Core Contributors are individuals who have demonstrated sustained, meaningful contribution to the project. They are expected to:
 
- Participate in key decisions through structured input
- Help moderate community spaces
- Review and respond to community proposals
- Uphold the values defined in the [Employee Handbook](../employee-handbook/)
Core Contributor status can be revoked for inactivity (180+ days) or conduct violations.
 
### 1.4 The Community
 
Every Corporate Survivor who participates in good faith is part of CubertCoin governance. The community has voice through:
 
- **GitHub Discussions** — proposals and structured debate
- **Discord** — real-time input and culture
- **Voting polls** — non-binding preference signals
Community input is taken seriously. It is not always decisive. This is, structurally, exactly like a real job.

## 📋 Article II — Decision-Making Process
 
*How things get approved around here.*
 
### 2.1 Decision Categories
 
Not all decisions are equal. CubertCoin uses three decision tiers:
 
| Category | Examples | Process |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **Operational** | Posting schedule, minor content, event coordination | Founder or Core Contributor decides unilaterally |
| **Strategic** | Roadmap changes, new partnerships, treasury spending | Community input → Core Contributor review → Founder decision |
| **Constitutional** | Token launch, Charter amendments, major brand changes | Extended community input → explicit Founder approval required |
 
### 2.2 The Proposal Process
 
Any community member may propose a Strategic or Constitutional decision by opening a **[GitHub Discussion](https://github.com/CubertCoin/cubertcoin/discussions)** under the `Proposals` category.
 
A valid proposal must include:
 
1. **Title** — clear and specific
2. **Problem Statement** — what issue does this address?
3. **Proposed Solution** — what change is being requested?
4. **Impact Assessment** — who does this affect and how?
5. **Alternatives Considered** — what other options were evaluated?
Proposals missing any of the five sections will be returned for revision. This is not gatekeeping. This is quality control.
 
### 2.3 Timelines
 
| Decision Type | Community Input | Review Period | Decision Deadline |
|---------------|----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Strategic | 7 days | 3 days | 14 days from proposal |
| Constitutional | 14 days | 7 days | 30 days from proposal |
 
If a decision is not made within the deadline, the proposal is automatically tabled and may be resubmitted after 30 days.
 
### 2.4 Voting
 
Voting on proposals is non-binding and advisory. It informs decisions — it does not make them.
 
Voting occurs via Discord polls or GitHub Discussions reactions. Minimum participation for a vote to be considered meaningful: **25 unique participants** (threshold subject to revision as community grows).
 
### 2.5 Founder Veto
 
The Founder may veto any proposal at any time. Veto decisions will be communicated publicly with a brief rationale where possible, out of respect for the community's time and effort.
 
This power exists to protect the project. It is expected to be used sparingly.

## 🗺️ Article III — Roadmap Governance
 
*The career ladder is not decorative.*
 
The official [Roadmap](../roadmap/) is maintained by the Founder around the Career Ladder structure — Intern through Executive. Core Contributors may propose additions. Community members may signal interest via GitHub Discussions.
 
Changes to the Roadmap follow the **Strategic** process. Status updates within existing items (moving a milestone from Pending to In Progress) are Operational and require no formal process.
 
In cases where external circumstances require immediate changes, the Founder may adjust the Roadmap without a formal proposal. Such changes will be announced publicly within 48 hours.

## 💰 Article IV — Treasury Governance
 
*The budget, finally approved.*
 
The Corporate Treasury holds 15% of total $CUBE supply, as defined in the [Tokenomics](../tokenomics/) document. It is governed by multi-sig — no single party can move funds unilaterally.

> At this stage, the multisig is self-custodied by the Founder across separate devices, per the configuration disclosed in the [Tokenomics](../tokenomics/) document. Regardless of signer configuration, liquidity and team allocations are additionally protected by immutable on-chain lock/vesting contracts that no signer — including the Founder — can bypass before unlock.
 
### Spending Authority
 
| Expense Type | Approval Required |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Operational (infrastructure, tools) | Founder |
| Community initiatives and grants | Core Contributor input + Founder approval |
| Strategic allocations (partnerships, listings) | Full proposal process |
 
All treasury movements above a minimum threshold (to be defined at launch) will be disclosed publicly within 7 days of execution. The Corporate Survivors have a right to know where the budget went.

## 👔 Article V — Intellectual Property
 
*What belongs to Cubert Coin belongs to all of us. Except when it doesn't.*
 
The following are protected assets of CubertCoin:
 
| Asset | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Cubert Coin** | The project name in all forms |
| **$CUBE** | The official token ticker |
| **Cubert** | The mascot character and all visual representations |
| **Proof of Burnout** | The core concept and consensus mechanism name |
| **"Another Meeting. Another Token."** | Official tagline |
| **Visual Identity** | Logos, color palette, and design system |
 
Usage guidelines for all visual assets are defined in the [Brand Book](../brand-book/). This Charter defines ownership — the Brand Book defines application.
 
### ⚠️ Unauthorized Use
 
The following are not permitted without explicit written approval from the Founder:
 
- Commercial use of the Cubert character or CubertCoin name
- Creation of derivative tokens, coins, or projects using CubertCoin IP
- Impersonation of official CubertCoin channels or representatives
- Use of the $CUBE ticker for any other token or project
Reports of IP violations should be submitted via official channels.

## 🛡️ Article VI — Community Standards and Moderation
 
*Conference Room B has rules.*
 
Conduct standards are defined in the [Employee Handbook](../employee-handbook/). This Charter does not repeat them. They are filed separately, in a folder you probably haven't opened yet.
 
### Moderation Authority
 
Core Contributors and designated moderators may remove content, issue warnings, and temporarily suspend access. The Founder has authority to permanently ban individuals from all CubertCoin community spaces. All moderation decisions may be appealed via official channels.
 
### 🚨 Scam and Fraud Response
 
CubertCoin operates a zero-tolerance policy for scams, impersonation, and fraud — particularly regarding $CUBE contract address impersonation.
 
Upon identification of a scam:
 
1. Core Contributors flag and document the incident
2. Community warning issued within **2 hours** across all channels
3. Founder reviews and issues official statement within **24 hours**
4. Incident logged in a public registry (to be established at launch)
> ⚠️ The official contract address will only ever be published via: [cubertcoin.com](https://www.cubertcoin.com), [@cubertcoin](https://x.com/cubertcoin) on X, the official [Discord](https://discord.gg/UqJwHACWhz), and the official [Telegram](https://t.me/cubertcoin). Any other source is unauthorized.

## 📝 Article VII — Amendments
 
*How to change the rules without calling a meeting about it.*
 
This Charter may be amended by the Founder at any time following the **Constitutional** process defined in Article II — meaning community input is required before any change is finalized. Amendments take effect 7 days after announcement.
 
Emergency amendments (legal, security, or operational risks) may take effect immediately. The community will be notified within 24 hours with a full explanation.
