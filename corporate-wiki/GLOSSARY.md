# 📖 Corporate Glossary
 
> *"All terms have been reviewed and approved by the Corporate Survivors Department.*
> *No jargon was harmed in the making of this document."*
 
This glossary exists because crypto has too much unexplained terminology — and Corporate Survivors deserve to understand exactly what they're getting into.
 
Terms are listed alphabetically. Each entry includes a technical definition and a Cubert translation for those who've spent more time in spreadsheets than on the blockchain.
 
---
 
## A
 
### Airdrop
**Technical:** A distribution of tokens sent directly to wallet addresses, usually for free, as part of a promotional or community-building campaign.
 
**Cubert translation:** Free tokens in your inbox. Like a company swag bag, but potentially worth something. CubertCoin has no planned airdrops — every $CUBE in existence was minted at launch and allocated transparently.
 
---
 
### Authority
**Technical:** In Solana's SPL Token standard, an authority is a permission assigned to a wallet that allows it to perform specific actions on a token — such as minting new tokens (Mint Authority) or freezing wallets (Freeze Authority).
 
**Cubert translation:** Think of it as admin access. We've made sure the right authorities are either revoked or never existed — so nobody can abuse them. Not even us.
 
---
 
## B
 
### Blockchain
**Technical:** A distributed, immutable ledger that records all transactions across a decentralized network of computers. Once data is written, it cannot be altered.
 
**Cubert translation:** A spreadsheet that nobody owns, everyone can read, and nobody can edit. The opposite of the one your manager keeps changing the day before a deadline.
 
---
 
### Burn
**Technical:** The permanent removal of tokens from circulation by sending them to an address nobody controls (a "burn address"), reducing total supply.
 
**Cubert translation:** Deleting tokens forever. CubertCoin has a fixed supply of 1,000,000,000 $CUBE — no burns planned, no supply games.
 
---
 
## C
 
### Cliff Period
**Technical:** In a vesting schedule, the cliff is an initial waiting period during which no tokens are released. After the cliff ends, vesting begins according to the defined schedule.
 
**Cubert translation:** The probation period. You don't get anything until you've proven you're in it for the long haul. Our team has a 6-month cliff — nobody cashes out on day one.
 
---
 
### Community Growth Fund
**Technical:** A dedicated allocation of tokens reserved for community-building activities such as campaigns, contests, partnerships, and ecosystem expansion.
 
**Cubert translation:** The budget for actually making things happen. 10% of $CUBE supply — 100,000,000 tokens — set aside to reward genuine participation and grow the Corporate Survivors community.
 
---
 
### Contract Address
**Technical:** The unique on-chain identifier for a token or smart contract on the blockchain. Used to look up the token on explorers, add it to wallets, and verify its authenticity.
 
**Cubert translation:** The token's employee ID. Always verify you're using the real one.
 
$CUBE Contract Address: `8mxQ2UGnxeRNczWD3g2b9HURc7iudw3sYuvef8QqPtMC` — [View on Solscan](https://solscan.io/token/8mxQ2UGnxeRNczWD3g2b9HURc7iudw3sYuvef8QqPtMC)
 
---
 
### Corporate Survivor
**Technical:** N/A — this one's ours.
 
**Cubert translation:** Anyone who has ever survived a meeting that should have been an email, a performance review that ignored their actual work, or a KPI that made no sense. If you've lived it, you're one of us.
 
---
 
## D
 
### DEX (Decentralized Exchange)
**Technical:** A peer-to-peer trading platform that operates without a central authority. Users trade directly from their wallets using smart contracts and liquidity pools.
 
**Cubert translation:** A stock exchange with no middleman, no lunch breaks, and no hold music. $CUBE will launch on a Solana DEX.
 
---
 
### Decimals
**Technical:** The number of decimal places a token supports, determining its smallest divisible unit. $CUBE uses 9 decimals, meaning 1 $CUBE = 1,000,000,000 base units.
 
**Cubert translation:** How many ways you can split a single token. $CUBE has 9 decimals — precise enough to survive any budget meeting.
 
---
 
## E
 
### Explorer
**Technical:** A web tool that allows anyone to look up transactions, wallet addresses, and token data on a blockchain. Common Solana explorers include Solscan, SolanaFM, and RugCheck.
 
**Cubert translation:** The audit trail. Everything that happens on-chain is visible on an explorer — wallet balances, transaction history, token details, lock status. We link to all relevant explorer pages in our [Tokenomics](./tokenomics/) documentation. No hiding.
 
---
 
## F
 
### Freeze Authority
**Technical:** A permission that allows the token issuer to freeze specific wallets, preventing them from transferring tokens.
 
**Cubert translation:** The ability to lock someone's wallet. We never had this — it was never set when $CUBE was created. Your tokens are yours. Nobody can freeze them. Not even us.
 
---
 
### FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)
**Technical:** The anxiety that others are benefiting from an opportunity you're not participating in. In crypto, FOMO often drives impulsive buying decisions.
 
**Cubert translation:** That feeling when a token 10x's and you were in the meeting where someone was sharing their screen for 20 minutes to open a PDF. We can't promise returns. But we can promise transparency.
 
---
 
### FUD (Fear, Uncertainty, and Doubt)
**Technical:** Negative or misleading information spread about a project, often to manipulate sentiment or price.
 
**Cubert translation:** The Monday morning meeting of crypto. Everything is a crisis, nothing is confirmed, and productivity drops to zero. When in doubt, check the on-chain facts.
 
---
 
## G
 
### Gas Fee
**Technical:** A small fee paid to the network to process a transaction. On Solana, these fees are extremely low — fractions of a cent.
 
**Cubert translation:** The cost of doing business on the blockchain. On Solana, it's basically a rounding error. Unlike your corporate expense report, which requires three approvals and a PDF.
 
---
 
## H
 
### Holder
**Technical:** Any wallet address that holds a non-zero balance of a specific token.
 
**Cubert translation:** A token owner. The more holders, the more distributed and decentralized the project. Currently, $CUBE has a small number of holders — this grows at launch.
 
---
 
## I
 
### Immutable
**Technical:** Describes data or contracts that cannot be changed after they are created. On-chain locks and revoked authorities are immutable.
 
**Cubert translation:** Set in stone. Unlike your job description, which somehow keeps expanding without a salary adjustment.
 
---
 
## L
 
### Liquidity
**Technical:** The availability of tokens in a trading pool that allows buyers and sellers to execute trades without significant price impact.
 
**Cubert translation:** The fuel that makes trading possible. Without liquidity, nobody can buy or sell. With locked liquidity, nobody can pull the rug either.
 
---
 
### Liquidity Lock
**Technical:** A mechanism that prevents the liquidity provider from withdrawing liquidity from a pool for a defined period, typically enforced by a smart contract.
 
**Cubert translation:** We put the liquidity in a vault and threw away the key — for 12 months. Even we can't touch it before the unlock date. That's the point.
 
---
 
### Liquidity Pool
**Technical:** A smart contract holding two paired assets that enables decentralized trading. Users can swap tokens against the pool at algorithmically determined prices.
 
**Cubert translation:** The trading desk. $CUBE will have 70% of its supply — 700,000,000 tokens — added to a liquidity pool at launch and locked immediately.
 
---
 
## M
 
### Market Cap
**Technical:** The total value of a token's circulating supply, calculated as: price × circulating supply.
 
**Cubert translation:** How much the project is "worth" on paper. Like a performance review score — it goes up and down and rarely reflects the actual work being done.
 
---
 
### Memecoin
**Technical:** A cryptocurrency primarily driven by community sentiment, internet culture, and social momentum rather than technical utility or institutional backing.
 
**Cubert translation:** A token powered by vibes, shared identity, and the belief that enough people care about the same thing. $CUBE is a community token — not a memecoin, but we understand the comparison.
 
---
 
### Mint
**Technical:** The act of creating new tokens on the blockchain. Once minted, tokens exist as on-chain assets.
 
**Cubert translation:** Printing tokens. We minted 1,000,000,000 $CUBE — the entire supply — at once. No more will ever be created.
 
---
 
### Mint Authority
**Technical:** The permission that allows a wallet to create (mint) additional tokens. Revoking it permanently fixes the total supply.
 
**Cubert translation:** The ability to print more tokens. Active since June 2026 — will be revoked permanently at launch. After that, 1,000,000,000 $CUBE is all there will ever be. No exceptions, no overrides.
 
---
 
### Multisig (Multi-Signature Wallet)
**Technical:** A wallet that requires multiple private key signatures to authorize a transaction, reducing single-point-of-failure risk.
 
**Cubert translation:** A wallet that needs more than one person to approve spending. Like requiring two managers to sign off on an expense — except this one actually makes sense. Our treasury uses a 2-of-3 multisig via Squads Protocol.
 
---
 
## N
 
### NFT (Non-Fungible Token)
**Technical:** A unique, non-interchangeable token on the blockchain representing ownership of a specific digital asset.
 
**Cubert translation:** A one-of-a-kind digital item. $CUBE is not an NFT — it's a fungible token, meaning every $CUBE is identical and interchangeable, like dollars.
 
---
 
## O
 
### On-Chain
**Technical:** Data, transactions, or operations that are recorded directly on the blockchain and are publicly verifiable.
 
**Cubert translation:** The receipts. Everything on-chain is permanent, public, and verifiable. No "my manager said so" — just math and cryptography.
 
---
 
## P
 
### Proof of Burnout
**Technical:** N/A — this one's ours.
 
**Cubert translation:** The founding thesis of Cubert Coin. The idea that surviving corporate life — the meetings, the KPIs, the performance reviews, the emails that should have been silence — is itself a form of work that deserves recognition.
 
The token is called $CUBE. The blockchain is Solana. The community is Corporate Survivors. But Proof of Burnout is the reason any of this exists.
 
---
 
## R
 
### Rug Pull
**Technical:** A malicious exit by a project's founders — typically by draining liquidity from a pool or selling a large token allocation — leaving investors with worthless tokens.
 
**Cubert translation:** The crypto equivalent of a company shutting down on a Friday afternoon with no notice. We've structured $CUBE specifically to make this impossible: liquidity locked for 12 months, team tokens vesting over 18 months, treasury under multisig. Verify everything on-chain.
 
---
 
## S
 
### Seed Phrase
**Technical:** A sequence of 12 or 24 random words that serves as the master key to a crypto wallet. Anyone who has your seed phrase has full control of your wallet and all its assets.
 
**Cubert translation:** The one password you must never share. Not with admins. Not with bots. Not with us. Not with anyone claiming to be from support. If someone asks for your seed phrase, it's a scam. Always.
 
---
 
### Smart Contract
**Technical:** Self-executing code deployed on a blockchain that automatically enforces the terms of an agreement without requiring a trusted intermediary.
 
**Cubert translation:** A contract that executes itself, with no HR department needed. Our vesting and liquidity locks are enforced by smart contracts — not promises.
 
---
 
### Solana
**Technical:** A high-performance blockchain known for fast transaction speeds (sub-second finality) and extremely low fees, making it ideal for DeFi and community tokens.
 
**Cubert translation:** The office building where $CUBE lives. Fast, cheap, and reliable — everything a corporate environment isn't.
 
---
 
### SPL Token (Classic)
**Technical:** The standard token format on the Solana blockchain, equivalent to ERC-20 on Ethereum. SPL Token Classic is the most audited, widely supported, and compatible standard available.
 
**Cubert translation:** The standard contract. We chose SPL Token Classic because it's simple, battle-tested, and doesn't require extensions we don't need. No hidden complexity.
 
---
 
### Supply
**Technical:** The total number of tokens that exist. Fixed supply means no new tokens can ever be created.
 
**Cubert translation:** How many tokens there are in total. $CUBE has a fixed supply of 1,000,000,000 — one billion tokens, forever. No inflation, no surprises.
 
---
 
## T
 
### Tax (Buy/Sell Tax)
**Technical:** A percentage fee automatically deducted from token transactions, often used to fund project treasuries or liquidity.
 
**Cubert translation:** A hidden fee on every trade. $CUBE has 0% buy tax and 0% sell tax. What you trade is what you get. No deductions, no withholdings, no surprises.
 
---
 
### Ticker
**Technical:** A short symbol used to identify a token on exchanges and explorers, similar to a stock ticker symbol.
 
**Cubert translation:** The token's nickname. Ours is $CUBE. You'll see it on Solscan, Jupiter, Raydium, and eventually everywhere Corporate Survivors gather.
 
---
 
### Token
**Technical:** A digital asset created on an existing blockchain. Unlike coins (which are native to their blockchain), tokens are built on top of it.
 
**Cubert translation:** A digital asset that lives on the blockchain. $CUBE is a token on Solana — it's not a coin, not a stock, not a promise. It's a community asset.
 
---
 
### Treasury
**Technical:** A reserve of tokens or funds controlled by a project, typically used for operations, development, and strategic decisions.
 
**Cubert translation:** The company budget. 15% of $CUBE supply — 150,000,000 tokens — is held in the Corporate Treasury under a 2-of-3 multisig. Nothing moves without multiple approvals.
 
---
 
## V
 
### Vesting
**Technical:** A schedule that releases tokens gradually over time, preventing immediate sale and aligning long-term incentives.
 
**Cubert translation:** Delayed gratification. Our team tokens unlock slowly over 18 months — 6-month cliff, then 12-month linear vesting. Nobody exits early. Not even Cubert.
 
---
 
### Volatility
**Technical:** The degree of price fluctuation of an asset over time. Crypto markets are generally considered highly volatile.
 
**Cubert translation:** How much the price swings. Crypto is volatile. $CUBE will be volatile. This is not financial advice — this is a warning and a disclaimer wrapped in corporate humor.
 
---
 
## W
 
### Wallet
**Technical:** A software or hardware tool that stores private keys and allows users to interact with the blockchain — sending, receiving, and holding tokens.
 
**Cubert translation:** Your crypto bank account. You control it. Nobody else should. Never share your seed phrase or private key with anyone — not bots, not admins, not us.
 
---
 
### Whitepaper
**Technical:** A document that outlines a project's vision, technology, tokenomics, and roadmap.
 
**Cubert translation:** The business plan. Ours is called The Cubert Thesis — available at [thesis/](./cubert-thesis/).
